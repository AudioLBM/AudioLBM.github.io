Audio Super-Resolution with Latent Bridge Models

Anonymous Authors · Under Submission

Abstract

We present a new framework for universal speech super-resolution based on latent Schrödinger bridge models. Our approach uses a cascaded structure to progressively reconstruct 192kHz audio from arbitrary low-resolution inputs. By modeling transitions in latent space rather than waveform space, our method reduces synthesis complexity and achieves high fidelity across diverse frequency bands. We evaluate the approach in both single-step and multi-step upsampling pipelines, demonstrating strong generalization and realistic reconstruction on high-frequency content.
